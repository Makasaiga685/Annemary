Summary

AnnMary Vikatoria Raduva is a passionate and proactive climate activist from Fiji, committed to environmental conservation and climate justice. Her journey began with the "Say No to Balloon Releasing" campaign in 2018, addressing the detrimental effects of balloon releases on marine life. Facing initial resistance, AnnMary leveraged social media to amplify her message, leading to widespread support and awareness. ​

Recognizing the power of collective action, she founded the Young Eco Champions Network, engaging youth under 18 in environmental initiatives such as mangrove planting, significantly contributing to Fiji's reforestation efforts. ​ AnnMary's dedication earned her a place at the United Nations Climate Summit in New York in 2019, where she represented Fijian youth and voiced the concerns of Pacific Islanders facing the immediate impacts of climate change. ​ In response to the compounded challenges of the COVID-19 pandemic and Cyclone Harold in 2020, AnnMary and her sister initiated the Lagilagi Relief Drive, assembling and distributing dignity kits to women and girls in need, highlighting her commitment to social issues intersecting with environmental crises. ​ Continuing her activism, AnnMary led a significant clean-up at Wailoaloa Beach in 2022, demonstrating her hands-on approach to environmental stewardship. ​ AnnMary's unwavering dedication and leadership exemplify the impactful role youth can play in driving environmental and social change, making her a prominent figure in the Pacific's climate activism landscape
